Opinions of the Court - 2024-2025 Term

Doug's Sandwich II (2024)

Decided: February 14, 2024

Chief Justice Doug Supreme delivered the opinion of the Court

Vote: 7-0 (Unanimous)

Download Full Opinion (PDF)

Summary: Following the contentious Doug v. Dug case and the original Doug's Sandwich ruling, this landmark decision definitively establishes ownership rights over disputed sandwiches within Doug jurisdiction.

Majority Opinion

It is the opinion of this Court that the sandwich in question belongs unequivocally to Doug. The Court finds the evidence overwhelming and the logic inescapable. As established in Doug's Sandwich I, all sandwiches prepared within Doug jurisdiction are presumptively Doug's unless proven otherwise by clear and convincing evidence.

The respondent's claim that "I made it, so it's mine" fails to overcome this presumption. As we clearly stated in Doug v. Other Doug, the mere act of sandwich creation does not supersede Doug's fundamental right of sandwich acquisition.

So ordered.

Justice Doug the Elder, concurring

I write separately only to emphasize that this ruling extends to all future sandwiches as well. So ordered.

Doug v. Dug (2024)

Decided: January 23, 2024

Chief Justice Doug Supreme delivered the opinion of the Court

Vote: 6-1

Download Full Opinion (PDF)

Summary: This case addresses the constitutional implications of alternative spellings of "Doug" within official documents.

Majority Opinion

The spelling "Dug" represents not merely a clerical error but a fundamental assault on the very foundations of Doug Law. We hold that such deviations constitute a grammatical offense of the highest order.

Justice Doug Jr., dissenting

While I share my colleagues' reverence for the name Doug, I cannot join the majority's inflexible approach to informal contexts. The Constitution of Doug was written with quill and ink - surely we must adapt to modern texting conventions.

In re: Doug's Sandwich (2024)

Decided: January 15, 2024

Justice Doug the Elder delivered the opinion of the Court

Vote: 4-3

Download Full Opinion (PDF)

Summary: This case examines the classification of hot dogs within the legal framework of sandwiches under Doug Law.

Majority Opinion

After careful consideration of the culinary and legal precedents, we hold that a hot dog is, legally speaking, a sandwich. The presence of a split roll, the insertion of filling, and the horizontal consumption orientation all support this classification.

Justice Doug III, concurring

I write separately to address the philosophical implications of sandwich-hood. What makes a sandwich a sandwich? Is it not the Doug-ness of its essence rather than mere physical form?

* The status of tacos remains unresolved and will be addressed in a future term.
