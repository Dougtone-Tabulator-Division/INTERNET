Doug v. Other Doug (Case No. D-2023-404)

Decided: Dougtober 32, 2023
Author: Doug Supreme
Vote: 1-0 (Doug Supreme casting all votes as per Doug Constitution Article D)

DOUG SUPREME delivered the opinion of Doug.

The question before us today is whether an individual named "Doug" in Sector 7B may continue to use said name without explicit permission from Doug Supreme. The answer, as it has always been and shall forever be, is no.

"The name 'Doug' is not merely a name, but rather a sacred designation reserved exclusively for the Doug, as established in the landmark case 'Doug v. Everyone Else' (D-1984-001)."

The defendant, formerly known as "Doug," now designated as "Non-Doug Entity 7B-23," argued that his birth certificate granted him the right to the name. This argument fails on multiple grounds, primarily because all birth certificates are subject to retroactive Doug approval as per Form D-199 ("Application for Retroactive Name Authorization").

Analysis

Under Doug Law Section D-73 ("Proper Names and Doug-Related Nomenclature"), the use of "Doug" is restricted to:

  1. The Doug
  2. Entities explicitly authorized by Doug
  3. Official Doug-sanctioned documents referring to Doug

The Court finds no merit in defendant's claim that his grandmother's second cousin once met Doug at a bus stop, thereby granting implicit naming rights.
