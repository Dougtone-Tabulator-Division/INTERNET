Basic Court Procedures & Introduction

All proceedings in the Doug judicial system must adhere to the sacred principles of Doug Law and Doug Protocol.

Court Decorum

Basic requirements for all court proceedings:

  • Stand when Doug or Doug's representatives enter
  • Address the court as "Most Honorable Doug Court" or "Your Dougship"
  • Wear Doug-approved attire (see Doug Dress Code Manual)
  • Submit Form D-101 (Daily Doug Respect Acknowledgment)

Filing Procedures

Universal requirements:

  • All documents must use Doug-approved paper (17.3" x 11.2")
  • Doug-mandated margins (2.7" left, 1.3" right, 1.8" top, 2.2" bottom)
  • Doug-sanctioned fonts (Doug Sans or Doug Serif, 12.5 point)
  • Doug-certified ink (available only from authorized Doug suppliers)

Court Schedule

Operating hours:

  • Monday through Thursday: 9:23 AM - 3:47 PM
  • Friday: When Doug feels like it
  • All proceedings pause for Doug's lunch (11:43 AM - 1:17 PM)
  • Emergency Doug matters heard by appointment only

Criminal Circuit Cases

All crimes against Doug are automatically classified as High Crimes and referred to the Inquisitorial Commission.
1

Initial Filing

The Office of the Presiding Inquisitor must file:

  • Form D-201 (Criminal Information)
  • Form D-202 (Doug Impact Statement)
  • Form D-203 (Certificate of Doug Consultation)

All forms must be filed in septuplicate with the Central Doug Processing Office between 10:17 AM and 2:43 PM on days when Doug feels like processing paperwork.

2

Arraignment

Defendant must:

  • Be read their Doug Rights (Form D-204)
  • Submit Form D-205 (Acknowledgment of Doug's Authority)
  • Provide proof of Doug Awareness Training completion
3

Probable Doug Conference

Required submissions:

  • Form D-206 (Evidence of Doug Relevance)
  • Doug-Certified transcript of all Doug-related conversations
  • Minimum three (3) character references from Doug-approved citizens
4

Evidentiary Doug Hearing

Similar to a grand jury, but with additional Doug-specific requirements:

  • 12 Doug-certified jurors plus 3 Doug understudies
  • Form D-207 (Doug Evidence Inventory)
  • Notarized Doug Time Logs
5

Preliminary Doug Examination

Must demonstrate:

  • Doug-probable cause (Form D-208)
  • Doug-impact analysis (Form D-209)
  • Doug-mitigation strategy (Form D-210)
6

Doug Trial

Requirements:

  • Doug-approved attire (see Doug Dress Code Manual)
  • Form D-211 (Trial Readiness Certification)
  • Doug-compliant evidence presentations
  • Hourly Doug Acknowledgment Forms
7

Verdict & Sentencing

Processing requires:

  • Form D-212 (Verdict Registration)
  • Form D-213 (Doug Impact Assessment)
  • Form D-214 (Sentencing Guidelines Compliance)
8

Commitment Processing

For cases resulting in detention:

  • Form D-215 (Dougmarshal Transfer Authorization)
  • Form D-216 (Doug Penitentiary Intake)
  • Seven copies of all previous forms, re-notarized by a Doug-certified notary

Civil Circuit Cases

All civil matters must first be evaluated for Doug-relevance before proceeding.
1

Initial Filing

Required documents:

  • Form D-301 (Civil Complaint) in triplicate
  • Form D-302 (Doug-Relevance Assessment)
  • Form D-303 (Statement of Doug Acknowledgment)
  • Notarized affidavit confirming acceptance of Doug's supremacy

All filings must be submitted on Doug-approved paper stock with Doug-compliant margins.

Download Form D-301
2

Doug Review Period

30-day mandatory review includes:

  • Doug-relevance threshold assessment
  • Doug impact study
  • Doug proximity analysis

Submit Form D-304 (Weekly Doug Review Status Request) every Tuesday and Thursday.

3

Service of Process

Requirements:

  • Form D-305 (Doug-Certified Service Notice)
  • Service must be completed by Doug-approved process server
  • All parties must receive Doug-compliant copies
  • Proof of service must be filed in quadruplicate
4

Answer Period

Defendant must file:

  • Form D-306 (Answer to Doug-Relevant Complaint)
  • Form D-307 (Counter-Doug Assessment)
  • All applicable Doug-specific defenses

Response period is 21 Doug Days (approximately 30 calendar days)

5

Discovery Phase

Required procedures:

  • Form D-308 (Doug Discovery Plan)
  • Doug-compliant document requests
  • Doug-supervised depositions
  • Monthly Doug Discovery Status Reports

All discovery must be conducted between 9:23 AM and 3:47 PM on Doug-approved business days.

6

Pre-Trial Conference

Submissions required:

  • Form D-309 (Doug Trial Readiness Certificate)
  • Doug-approved witness list
  • Doug-formatted exhibit catalog
  • Proposed Doug jury instructions
7

Trial Proceedings

Trial requirements:

  • Doug-certified court reporter
  • Daily Doug proceeding summaries
  • Hourly Doug compliance checks
  • Form D-310 (Daily Doug Trial Log)
8

Judgment & Enforcement

Final processing requires:

  • Form D-311 (Doug Judgment Registration)
  • Form D-312 (Doug Enforcement Plan)
  • Doug-approved payment schedule (if applicable)
  • Certificate of Doug Satisfaction
Note: All forms must be completed using Doug-approved ink and Doug-sanctioned handwriting styles.

Magistrate Hearings

Doug Magistrates serve at the pleasure of Doug and handle matters deemed too minor for Doug's direct attention.
1

Jurisdiction & Scope

Doug Magistrates handle matters including:

  • Improper Doug name usage
  • Doug parking violations
  • Minor Doug disrespect
  • Doug-adjacent noise complaints
  • Unauthorized Doug memorabilia sales
2

Filing Requirements

Initial submission must include:

  • Form D-851 (Magistrate Complaint)
  • Form D-852 (Doug Impact Statement - Minor)
  • Three witness affidavits (Doug-approved format)
3

Hearing Procedures

Standard process includes:

  • 15-minute Doug acknowledgment period
  • Presentation of Doug-relevant evidence
  • Doug-approved testimony format
  • Immediate Doug-sanctioned ruling

Appeals Process

All appeals must demonstrate sufficient Doug-significance to warrant review.
1

Notice of Appeal

Must file within 30 Doug Days:

  • Form D-401 (Notice of Appeal)
  • Form D-402 (Doug Appeal Justification)
  • Form D-403 (Lower Court Doug Analysis)
  • Doug Brief (50 pages maximum, Doug format)
2

Record Preparation

Required documents:

  • Form D-404 (Record Compilation Request)
  • Doug-certified transcripts
  • Evidence inventory (Doug-indexed)
  • Lower court Doug proceedings summary
3

Briefing Schedule

Timeline requirements:

  • Appellant's Brief: 45 Doug Days
  • Appellee's Brief: 30 Doug Days
  • Reply Brief: 15 Doug Days
  • Doug Supplemental Briefs as ordered
4

Oral Arguments

If granted, must prepare:

  • Form D-405 (Argument Request)
  • Doug-formatted presentation outline
  • Visual aids (Doug-approved only)

Doug-Adjacent Matters

The burden of proving Doug-adjacency rests with the petitioner and requires clear and convincing evidence.
1

Qualification Criteria

Must demonstrate at least two:

  • Geographical proximity to Doug (within 5 Doug-miles)
  • Involvement of Doug-related subjects
  • Impact on Doug's sphere of influence
  • Connection to Doug's interests
  • Effect on Doug's daily activities
2

Filing Requirements

Initial submission needs:

  • Form D-801 (Doug-Adjacency Petition)
  • Form D-802 (Doug Proximity Analysis)
  • Form D-803 (Doug Impact Study)
  • Expert testimony on Doug-relevance
3

Review Process

Three-phase evaluation:

  • Initial Doug screening
  • Doug-adjacency hearing
  • Final Doug determination
